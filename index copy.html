About

I am a sixth-year Ph.D. student in Statistics at Stanford University, advised by Prof. Wing Hung Wong. I also work with Prof. Emmanuel Candes. My research broadly focuses on statistical inference on large scale data sets. One approach is statistical learning via non-parametric density estimation. In particular, I am interested in cases when the dimension is moderately large, and try to use piece-wise constant functions summarized by binary trees to roughly capture the interference among variables. I'm also interested in multiple testing problems.

Before moving to the Bay Area, I was an undergrad at Tsinghua University, China, where I obtained a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Physics and a Minor in Economics.

Outside research, I enjoy Chinese calligraphy, skiing and hiking.
